# Frontispiece

## About the Guide
CycloneDX is a modern standard for the software supply chain, ratified as [ECMA-424](https://ecma-international.org/publications-and-standards/standards/ecma-424/) by Ecma International.

The content in this guide results from continuous community feedback and input from leading experts in the software
supply chain security field. This guide would not be possible without valuable feedback from the CycloneDX Industry
Working Group (IWG), the CycloneDX Core Working Group (CWG), the CycloneDX Hardware Feature Working Group (FWG),
Ecma International Technical Committee 54, and a global network of contributors and supporters.

This guide covers the CycloneDX hardware capability: the inventory, materials, sourcing, substitution, origin, certification, and part-authenticity models that describe physical products and the parties behind them. It is written for CycloneDX v2.0 and is informative, introducing no requirements of its own. Hardware and manufacturing terms are defined where they first appear, and the glossary in Appendix A collects them, because readers arrive from both the software and the manufacturing side and the two communities often use different words for the same thing.

Readers new to CycloneDX can start with Hardware Concepts and Terminology, continue with the chapter matching the task at hand, and treat the appendices as reference material. The Introduction and HBOM Use Cases chapters are self-contained and carry no JSON, so policy and executive readers can stop there. Manufacturers and suppliers should add Inventorying a Hardware Assembly and Alternates and Substitution, security teams should add Substantiating Part Identity, and compliance teams should add Hardware Certifications and Origin and Foreign Influence Analysis, and procurement and vendor risk teams should add Identifying Manufacturers and Suppliers. The CycloneDX 2.0 documentation covers the foundation: the specification format, the party model in full, and bom-ref and BOM-Link mechanics. The Authoritative Guide to SBOM covers software inventory, licensing, and pedigree, and all organizations, products, and people in the worked examples are fictional.
